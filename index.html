About me

I am a Reader and a Leverhulme Early Career Fellow at the Department of Mathematical Sciences, University of Bath. I belong to the Numerical Analysis group, the Bath Imaging Group and am a co-director of Centre for Mathematics and Algorithms for Data (MAD). Moreover, I am also a fellow at the Institute for Mathematical Innovation

My research lies on the interface of optimization, inverse imaging problems and machine learning where I develop algorithms that are efficient enough for large-scale applications with proven convergence guarantees and robustness. My work bridges mathematical theory to various applications including medical imaging, material sciences, biology and geophysics.

Claire Delplancke (PostDoc since 01/2020)

Before being a Research Associate at the University of Bath, Claire did a PhD on stochastic processes in Toulouse, France and a first post-doc in Santiago, Chile. She enjoys travels and good reads. She’s a member of the PET++ project and is interested in Stochastic Algorithms used in Inverse Problems from medical and seismic tomography.

Margaret Duff (PhD student since 06/2019)

I am a PhD student as part of the Statistical Applied Mathematics CDT at the University of Bath. Before coming to Bath I completed a BA and MMath degrees at Emmanuel College, University of Cambridge. My research looks at how generative deep learning methods can be applied to inverse problems and imaging. It sits across mathematics and computer science and includes themes from numerical analysis and machine learning.

Eric Baruch Gutierrez Castillo (PhD student since 02/2019)

My name is Eric Baruch Gutierrez Castillo, MSc Applied Mathematics from Delft University of Technology, The Netherlands. Currently PhD student at University of Bath, Department of Mathematical Sciences, under the supervision of Dr. Matthias J. Ehrhardt. As part of the numerical analysis group, my research focuses on convergence of stochastic primal-dual algorithms for convex optimization, where I look into both convergence analysis and practical applications. Applications reach several disciplines, with a special interest in Imaging and Machine Learning.

Jarrod Williams (PostDoc, 12/2019-07/2021)

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate on a Faraday Institution project, working on Image Processing/Inverse problems arising in battery characterisation. In practice, my research combines techniques from machine learning and continuous optimisation, from neural networks to proximal methods. I am also interested in applied differential geometry (my earlier studies were in mathematical physics), particularly its applications in Geometry Processing. I spend a lot of my spare time either exploring the great outdoors or playing the violin (badly!).
